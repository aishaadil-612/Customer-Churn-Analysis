Customer Churn Analysis

Overview

Customer churn is a critical business metric that measures the rate at which customers stop using a company's services. This project analyzes customer behavior and subscription patterns to identify factors that contribute to customer churn and provide actionable insights for improving customer retention.

---

Dataset Information

The dataset contains **64,374 customer records** and **12 features** related to customer demographics, subscription details, service usage, and customer interactions.

### Features

| Feature           | Description                                       |
| ----------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| CustomerID        | Unique customer identifier                        |
| Age               | Customer age                                      |
| Gender            | Male/Female                                       |
| Tenure            | Duration of customer relationship                 |
| Usage Frequency   | Frequency of service usage                        |
| Support Calls     | Number of support calls made                      |
| Payment Delay     | Delay in payment completion                       |
| Subscription Type | Customer subscription plan                        |
| Contract Length   | Duration of contract                              |
| Total Spend       | Total amount spent by customer                    |
| Last Interaction  | Days since last interaction                       |
| Churn             | Customer churn status (0 = Retained, 1 = Churned) |

## Key Findings

### 1. Age Influences Churn

Customer age showed noticeable differences between churned and retained customers, indicating that age can be an important factor in customer retention strategies.

### 2. Contract Length Affects Retention

Customers with longer contract lengths demonstrated lower churn rates compared to customers on shorter contracts.

### 3. Payment Delays Increase Churn Risk

Customers with frequent payment delays were more likely to churn than customers who paid on time.

### 4. Support Calls Are Associated with Churn

Customers who made a higher number of support calls tended to have higher churn rates, suggesting dissatisfaction or service issues.

### 5. Customer Tenure Impacts Loyalty

Long-term customers were generally less likely to churn than newer customers.

### 6. Total Spending Reflects Customer Engagement

Customers with higher total spending often exhibited stronger retention and loyalty.

### 7. Usage Frequency Influences Retention

Customers who used the service more frequently were generally more likely to remain active subscribers.
